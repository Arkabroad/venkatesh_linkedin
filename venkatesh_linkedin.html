Day 1
Working a checkout for three years taught me something no data course does. Customers don't behave the way a clean dataset suggests they should. Someone buys the same five items every Tuesday for months, then switches to a completely different basket the week prices change on just one of them. No survey would catch that. No dashboard flags it unless someone is actually watching the till. I used to think retail and data analysis were two separate parts of my life. One paid the bills, the other was what I studied for. Now I think the checkout was the training ground. It taught me to notice the exception before I trust the average. What's one thing your current job taught you that no dashboard could?

Day 5
I finished a Master's in IT in an Australian classroom, then went back to a checkout the next week. Rent doesn't pause for job hunts. For a while I felt like I was standing still while the degree just sat there. Two banking roles in India, a fresh Australian qualification, and still swiping groceries five nights a week. What changed my mind was realising the checkout wasn't cancelling out the degree. It was adding a layer the degree couldn't teach on its own, reading people and patterns in real time, under pressure, with no time to double check anything. Most career gaps aren't gaps. They're just work nobody thought to put on a resume. Has your career ever moved sideways before it moved forward?

Day 10
82.4% of international graduates in Australia are now in full-time work within four months of finishing their degree. Three years ago that gap against domestic graduates was 8.1 points. It has nearly halved since. The story most of us tell ourselves, that the market doesn't want international talent, is getting harder to back up with current numbers. What's actually happening is slower and less dramatic: employers are getting more comfortable hiring people who studied here, and the graduates who move first are the ones benefiting. I'm one of the people betting that trend keeps going in Melbourne's data and analytics teams. Is the market actually closed to you, or have you just not tested it lately?
