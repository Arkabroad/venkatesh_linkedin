Day 1
Before my first job, I built a train ticket booking system in ASP.NET. Nobody asked me to. I just wanted to see if I could take a real, messy problem, people booking seats, holding fares, handling cancellations, and make it work end to end. That instinct hasn't changed. What has changed is the scale of problem I'm chasing. My capstone research pulled me into how AI models handle language, and I keep coming back to the same question I had building that booking system: where does this actually break, and why? Most job descriptions want you to list frameworks. Nobody asks what problem made you stay up late figuring it out. What's the last thing you built just because you wanted to know if you could?

Day 5
I finished a Master's in IT in an Australian classroom, then went back to a supermarket floor in Melbourne the next week. Rent doesn't pause for job hunts. For a while it felt like the degree was just sitting there, unused. Two banking placements in India, a fresh Australian qualification, still stacking shelves five nights a week. What changed my mind was realising the two weren't in competition. Writing code and reading a busy shop floor both come down to the same thing, staying accurate when there's no time to double check. Most career gaps aren't gaps. They're just work nobody thought to put on a resume. Has your career ever moved sideways before it moved forward?

Day 10
82.4% of international graduates in Australia are now in full-time work within four months of finishing their degree. Three years ago that gap against domestic graduates was 8.1 points. It has nearly halved since. The story most of us tell ourselves, that the market doesn't want international talent, is getting harder to back up with current numbers. What's actually happening is slower and less dramatic: employers are getting more comfortable hiring people who studied here, and the graduates who move first are the ones benefiting. I'm one of the people betting that trend keeps going in Melbourne's software and AI/ML teams. Is the market actually closed to you, or have you just not tested it lately?
